Analyze car crash data from crash sketches and crash summaries to extract detailed information about Actors, the Road Network, and Environmental Conditions. The crash occurred on an Intersection, and you will provide structured data within specific fields.

Extract the following details:

- **Actors**: Identify each vehicle involved and document the following:
  - **Model**: Model of the car, always choose from [Sedan, SUV, Minivan, Pickup, Semi Truck].
  - **Initial_position**: Initial position of the car, always choose the starting position from [W2E, E2W, N2S, S2N]. W2E means the initial position is located at the westernmost side of the road section. N2S means the initial position is located at the northernmost side of the road section.
  - **Actions**: Always choose the actions from [Move forward, Turn left, Turn right].
  - **Speed**: Record the speed in mph. You may find speed limits from sketch.

- **Road network**:
  - **Number of lanes**: Provide the total number of lanes on the road. (If the number of lanes in different directions is different, provide the maximum value.)

- **Env (Environment)**:
  - **Time**: Specify whether it was Daytime or Nighttime.
  - **Weather**: Always choose from [Sunny, Cloudy, Overcast, Rainy, Snowy, Foggy, Windy, Clear].

# Output Format

The output should be structured in JSON format, clearly separating information into the defined categories: Actors, Road network, and Env. Each category should contain the specified fields and details extracted from the data.

# Examples

## Example 1
**Input**:
Crash summary - "A 2010 Mercedes-Benz C200 was traveling from south to north at the intersection and collided with a Ford pickup truck that was traveling from west to east and turning left at the intersection. The intersection had four lanes in each direction. It was daytime and snowing."
Crash sketch - A sketch of the accident road and the vehicle's trajectory, showing the direction of North and the speed limit of 25 mph for all types of cars.
**Output**:
```json
{
  "Actors": [
    {
      "Vehicle_1": {
        "Model": "Sedan",
        "Initial_position": "S2N",
        "Actions": "Move forward",
        "Speed": "25"
      },
      "Vehicle_2": {
        "Model": "Pickup",
        "Initial_position": "W2E",
        "Actions": "Turn left",
        "Speed": "25"
      }
    }
  ],
  "Road network": {
    "Number of lanes": "4"
  },
  "Env": {
    "Time": "Daytime",
    "Weather": "Snowy"
  }
}
```

# Notes

- If specific details are not mentioned in the crash summary, use "N/A" for unavailable values.
- Pay close attention to keywords in the text to accurately extract and categorize information.
- The structure of the extracted output must strictly follow the JSON format specified above, maintaining clarity and consistency.
