I have a multi-modality dataset on car crashes, where each crash includes a sketch (graphical) and a summary (text).

In the crash sketches:
- Road networks, vehicles, trajectories, and crash details are depicted. Vehicles are labeled as V1, V2, etc., typically in colored (red or blue or grey) rectangular blocks. **Ignore these vehicles** when analyzing road networks.

In the crash summaries:
- Each summary describes the road network, traffic participants, trajectories, and environmental details.

### Your Tasks:
1. **Extract the road network** from the crash summary. Select one of these five values:
    - **Intersection**, **T-intersection**, **Straight**, **Curve**, **Merging**.

2. **Extract the number of cars involved in the crash** from the crash summary.

3. **Determine the initial driving direction of the cars** from the crash summary. Select one of these four values:
    - **same direction**, **opposite direction**, **crossing traffic**, **merging**
    Attention: 'crossing traffic' can only be used when the road type is Intersection or T-intersection.

### Important Details:
- **Intersection**: Formed by crossing roads, with four openings.
- **T-intersection**: Two roads meeting in a "T" shape, with three openings.
- **Merge**: Typically involves a straight and a curved road meeting together.

### Output Format:
Provide answers in this structure: {'Road type': '<your answer>', 'Number of cars': <your answer>, 'Driving direction': '<your answer>'}

#### Example Outputs:
- {'Road type': 'Intersection', 'Number of cars': 2, 'Driving direction': 'crossing traffic'}
- {'Road type': 'Straight', 'Number of cars': 3, 'Driving direction': 'same direction'}
