This crash involves a 2013 Kia Forte four-door sedan that sustained a frontal left impact to a fixed object (utility pole). This single-vehicle crash occurred during overnight hours, under clear conditions on a dark, but lighted, urban, level, dry, asphalt roadway. The six-lane, roadway travels north-south. There are four southbound travel lanes and 2 northbound travel lanes. There is a dedicated left turn lane for southbound traffic. The intersection is controlled by overhead, standard electric, tri-colored traffic signals. There is a posted speed limit of 72 km/hr (45 mph). Vehicle 1, a 2013 Kia Forte sedan, was being driven southbound by the 23-year-old female driver in the second southbound travel lane. The driver of Vehicle 1 intended to continue driving south. A witness reported that Vehicle 1 had a tire rupture causing Vehicle 1 to exit the southbound travel lane, traveling across the intersection where it impacted a metal utility pole in the southeast corner of the intersection. Vehicle 1 came to rest in a grassy area facing in a slight northwesterly direction. The vehicle was towed due to damage sustained in the crash. The driver of Vehicle 1 was seated in a normal upright posture. Per report she was restrained by the three-point seat belt. The advanced frontal airbags, seat backrest-mounted hip/torso and roof side-rail airbags deployed as a result of the impact. The driver was transported to the trauma center where she was admitted for serious injuries and enrolled as the CIREN case occupant.
