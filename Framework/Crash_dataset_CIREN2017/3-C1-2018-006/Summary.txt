This crash involves a 2014 Toyota Sienna minivan, modified for a mobility-impaired driver, that sustained a frontal impact to a fixed object. This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours, under clear conditions, on a five-lane industrial, asphalt roadway. The dry, level, five-lane road travels north/south without traffic controls with two lanes going northbound, two lanes going southbound, and a central turning lane. There is a posted speed limit of 56 kmph (35 mph). Vehicle 1, a 2014 Toyota Sienna, was being driven northbound by the 63-year-old male driver in the left northbound travel lane. The driver of V1 intended to continue driving north. The driver of V1 reportedly coughed causing V1 to veer left, traveling across the center turning lane, across the two southbound travel lanes, and then struck a fixed object (concrete bridge wall) with the frontal plane. Vehicle 1 came to rest with the driver’s side door against the concrete bridge wall facing north. The vehicle was towed due to damage sustained in the crash. The driver of V1 was seated in an upright posture in a wheelchair. Per report, the wheelchair was secured in the vehicle. The driver states he was restrained to his wheelchair by the lap strap, but did not have his chest strap secured. The advanced frontal and both side rail-mounted curtain airbags deployed as a result of the impact. The driver was transported to a trauma center where he was admitted for serious injuries. The 63-year-old male driver was enrolled as the CIREN case occupant.
