This crash involves a 2007 GMC Yukon large utility vehicle that sustained a frontal impact to a fixed object. This single-vehicle crash occurred during daylight hours under clear conditions on a two-lane rural asphalt roadway. The dry, level two-lane road travels north/south with one travel lane in each direction, without traffic controls. There is a posted speed limit of 72 km/hr (45 mph). Vehicle 1, a 2007 GMC Yukon, was being driven northbound by the 42-year-old male driver in the northbound travel lane. The driver of V1 intended to continue driving north. The driver failed to negotiate a right curve causing V1 to exit the travel lane to the left, travel across the southbound travel lane, then off the roadway on the left-hand side where it struck a fixed object (tree) with the frontal plane. Vehicle 1 came to rest off-road on a grassy surface facing in a slight northeasterly direction. The vehicle was towed due to damage sustained in the crash. The driver of V1 was seated in an unknown posture because he was asleep. The driver was wearing his three-point lap and shoulder restraint belt. The advanced frontal airbags deployed as a result of the impact. The driver was mechanically restrained (entrapped), was extricated, then transported to the trauma center where he was admitted for serious injuries. The 42-year-old male driver was enrolled as the CIREN case occupant.
