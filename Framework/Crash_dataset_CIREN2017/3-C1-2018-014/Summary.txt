This single vehicle crash involves a 2012 Mazda3 four-door sedan that sustained frontal impacts to several fixed objects. This single-vehicle crash occurred under dark, clear conditions on an unlighted, one-lane, one-way, rural, concrete, roadway. The dry, +3% uphill grade, one-lane, curved, exit ramp is without traffic controls. There is a posted speed limit of 89 km/hr (55 mph). Vehicle 1, a 2012 Mazda3, was being driven eastbound by the 23-year-old male driver (CIREN case subject). Vehicle 1 exited the roadway onto the single lane exit ramp that leads onto the westbound roadway. The driver of Vehicle 1 intended to merge onto the two-lane roadway and continue traveling westbound. Vehicle 1 exited the left side of the roadway in the right-hand curve and impacted a metal guardrail (Event 1) with its left frontal plane. Vehicle 1 continued traveling off the roadway in a northwesterly path where it struck a tree (Event 2) with its frontal plane. Vehicle 1 came to rest against the tree facing northwest. The vehicle was towed due to damage sustained in the crash. The driver and only occupant of Vehicle 1 was seated in a normal upright posture. Per report he was restrained by the three-point seat belt. The vehicle was equipped with advanced frontal airbags, seat backrest, and roof rail-mounted curtain airbags. The steering wheel airbag deployed as a result of the initial impact. The 23-year-old male driver sustained moderate injuries and was transported on land to a local trauma center where he was later enrolled as the CIREN case occupant.
